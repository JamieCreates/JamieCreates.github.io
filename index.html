There you sit at your computer. Your leg is bobbing up and down in your impatience, your assortment of mismatched pens rattling in the “World’s Best Boss” mug you bought for yourself at a yard sale that your spouse had dragged you to last weekend. The endless scroll through resumes, cover letters, and other documents makes you question how long you’ve been doing this. Hours? Days? It all blends together in an incoherent mess of letters and colors and you start thinking that perhaps it’s past time to take a vacation. Just as you have decided to make an emergency call to your therapist— a friend of yours who definitely isn’t actually a therapist— you freeze. You see another application; you think it might be the one. You slowly push your blue light blocking glasses back up your nose.
